Galaxy Digital Launches Tokenization of Its Stock

A First in U.S. Public Company Equity Tokenization

Galaxy Digital Inc., a Nasdaq-listed digital asset firm led by Mike Novogratz, has become the first U.S. public company to tokenize its actual Class A common shares, enabling shareholders to convert them into blockchain-based tokens with full equity rights intact.

Bridging Traditional Finance & Blockchain

Partnering with Superstate, an SEC-registered transfer agent, Galaxy launched tokenized shares on the Solana blockchain through the Opening Bell platform. These shares are backed 1:1 by real equity and confer full legal and economic rights, unlike synthetic alternatives.

  • Shareholders complete KYC with Superstate before accessing tokenized GLXY shares.
  • Tokenized shares can be held, sent, and received via compliant wallets.
  • Over 32,000 Galaxy shares have already been tokenized on Solana.

Unlocking Speed, Access, and Transparency

By tokenizing shares, Galaxy enables 24/7 global settlement and faster trading opportunities for institutional investors. The firm is also exploring how tokenized equities might trade through Automated Market Makers (AMMs).

Why It Matters

  • Historic Compliance: First Nasdaq-listed company issuing real equity as blockchain tokens.
  • Infrastructure Innovation: Proves that public equities can adopt blockchain for efficiency.
  • Industry Momentum: Sets precedent for wider adoption of tokenized securities in traditional markets.

Outlook

Galaxy’s initiative signals a new era where blockchain meets traditional finance. If successful, it could encourage other publicly traded companies to tokenize shares, offering both speed and transparency to global markets.
